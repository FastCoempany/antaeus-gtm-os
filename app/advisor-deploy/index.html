The Ask Formula
1. Context (2 sentences): What's the deal, what stage, what happened.
2. What you've tried (1-2 sentences): Shows you're not lazy.
3. The specific ask (1 sentence): What you need them to do.
4. The draft: The script they can customize and send.
5. The stakes (1 sentence): Why it matters.
Psychology of the Ask
Fear of seeming weak → Every enterprise sales leader uses executive engagement. This is standard ops at scale.

Fear of burning the relationship → Most advisors feel underutilized, not overutilized.

Fear of looking desperate → Investors expect stalls. A surgical ask with a pre-drafted script is operational maturity.

Fear of the ask being too big → The ask isn't "make a call." It's "send this 3-line email I've already drafted." Cognitive load determines action.
