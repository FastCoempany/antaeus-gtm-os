Four systems. No rail.

The page is time. The sections are ribbons. The cards have gravity. One item per zone breaks rank. And the left nav rail is gone — finding your way is the work itself, not a menu of rooms.

This is the four-system library, updated. Two things changed. First, the rail is dead. A persistent room-list down the left was the one thing that made the workspace read like every other CRM; in its place sits the Wayfinder bar — a thin top strip carrying where-you-were, where-you-are, and the one thing pulling next, with its reasoning one click away. Second, the voice is cleaned — every invented compound noun ("readout owner", "cast proof") is now a plain sentence a peer would say. Everything else holds: Pulse organizes the page along time, Ribbon threads the sections, Grounded gives cards their weight and their Signal / Reason / Move grammar, Offset lifts one item per zone.

09

The full icon system is its own owed sibling spec (charter §4.7); this is the discipline it ships under, plus a starter set. Every icon is a thin line glyph — 1.6px stroke, currentColor, on a 20px box — and it holds its place only by standing for a sacred noun or a verb the operator acts with. Default navy; an icon takes an accent color only when the thing it names is itself the accent (the send-move is orange, a signal is blue). No filled icons, no spot illustrations, no icon used purely to fill space.

The composition, in discipline

Four systems, four scales, one workspace — and no rail. They compose because they never fight over the same magnification, and they hold together only as long as each layer keeps its discipline. Overrun any one of them and the page returns to noise.

PULSE · page

Moves are authored, not logged. Quiet deals are surfaced deliberately. The past compresses; it never piles up.

RIBBON · section

Threads mark time-zones and groups only. Never a ribbon on a single card. Movement, not decoration.

GROUNDED · card

Anchored edges and gauges are reserved for cards whose state warrants weight. Grounding everything erases the meaning.

OFFSET · item

Exactly one offset per zone. The single most-pressured item breaks rank; the rest hold the line.

The un-nav, in discipline. No room-list down the left, ever. The Wayfinder bar — one strip, three cells — is the only persistent chrome in the product; it carries where-you-were, where-you-are, and what's-pulling, and it travels with you across rooms. Never a second strip, never a rail beneath it. Rooms are summoned through Ctrl+K, not browsed. The bar's reasoning opens inline, on an explicit click, never on hover.

End to end, not just the hero. Every component renders five conditions, not one — empty (why it matters + the one move that fills it), sparse (a real start, not a fault), loading (the shape holds), error (honest and recoverable), saved (it visibly mattered), per canon Part II §6. Quantity is prose first: the intervention board shows many without becoming a spreadsheet, progress is a ladder of real milestones, and the only chart the language admits is a single bounded-ratio meter with the sentence carrying the meaning — no multi-series charts, no pies, no decorative sparklines. Icons name the sacred nouns and the operator's verbs and nothing else; the full set is its own owed spec, but the discipline — thin line glyphs, semantic only, accent color only where the thing is the accent — holds now.

Canon preserved throughout. Bright field. DM Serif Display for headlines, stats, account names, and the system's read. Public Sans for body and controls. JetBrains Mono for ribbon labels, codes, and timestamps. Orange stays the rationed accent — the one dominant move per surface, the offset emphasis, the pulling cell. Every operator-facing string is a plain sentence a peer would say: no "readout owner," no "cast proof," no "verdict." The native primitives — Signal · Reason · Move · State · Evidence — are the object model; cards, ribbons, pulses, and the Wayfinder are how they render.
