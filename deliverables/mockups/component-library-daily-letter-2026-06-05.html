The idea

The Daily Letter. The workspace is correspondence. The system writes; the operator reads, acts inline, and signs off. No dashboard grid, no rooms competing for attention — one long-form document arrives every morning, addressed by name, dated, archived. Old letters stay; the inheritor reads them backward to learn the motion before they ever open a single room.

Motion comes from reading. The vertical scroll IS the motion. The eye descends through the day in the order the system thinks matters. Cards exist only as inline callouts that interrupt the flow when a decision needs to land — not as objects on a board the operator scans.

What stays canon. Bright field (cream-warmed). DM Serif Display for the salutation. Public Sans for body. JetBrains Mono for codes and timestamps. Orange for the dominant move, the canon color roles intact. Voice rule honored throughout — the letter sounds like a peer operator with B2B sales scars writing in plain English.
