The idea

The Working Object. The workspace surfaces one working object at a time — the deal you're thinking about, the brief you're composing, the proof you're casting — and gives it the visual space and depth to be done well. Around it: ambient context. What you were just looking at, recessive on the left. What's queued next, quiet on the right. The system has chosen what to put in the center based on pressure and your last move.

Motion is lateral. Each focus is a stop. Cmd-K moves to the next; clicking a queued item moves to it directly; finishing the recommended action moves automatically. The day is a sequence of working sessions, not a scan of a board. Sarah-day-90 cmd-Ks through her morning; the day-one operator clicks through the queue.

What stays canon. Bright field. DM Serif Display for the working object's name and the system's read of it. Public Sans for body. JetBrains Mono for codes and timestamps. Orange for the dominant move and the working eyebrow. All canon color roles intact; voice rule honored throughout — the system speaks like a peer operator naming what is real and recommending what to do next.
