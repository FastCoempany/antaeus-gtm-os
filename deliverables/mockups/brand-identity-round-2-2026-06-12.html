SECTION 2 · CROSSBAR + OVERHANG

Where the bar sits, how far the ground runs.

The crossbar is the gauge reading; lower = more loaded. The ground-line overhang is the "extends past its feet" of the pick; longer = more horizon, shorter = more pedestal. My read: P2 — crossbar at the round-1 height, overhang slightly longer so the ground reads as ground, not underline.

SECTION 3 · THE MARK AT 32 / 24 / 16

Does it survive the browser tab?

Small sizes need heavier strokes (the round-1 ratios: 4 at 24px, 5 at 16px) and at 16px the crossbar goes — three strokes on a 16-pixel box is one too many. The 16px mark is legs + ground only; it still reads as the Grounded A because the ground line is the signature, not the bar. Shown at true size next to a fake tab.
