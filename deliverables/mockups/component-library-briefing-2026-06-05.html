Pipeline risk is concentrated. Three deals share the same shape.

Three deals are slipping for the same underlying reason. Acme, Cordillera, and Stonebridge all share a late-stage shape where the economic buyer has gone silent or absent while a non-EB stakeholder reopens commercial questions. The pattern is not new — it shows up in three of the last six closed-lost deals on record — but it is acute this week. Below: the situation, the implication, the move.

1.
— Situation

Late-stage legal and pricing pressure has stalled three expansion deals.

Acme is at day 18 with procurement re-opening pricing. Cordillera is at day 47 with legal stalled on data residency. Stonebridge is at day 22 with the champion potentially out of seat. Each one has a different presenting issue. None of them is the real problem.

2.
— Implication

The common failure mode is executive absence.

None of the three accounts has a live economic-buyer thread. The presenting issues — pricing, legal, champion turnover — all look like operational friction, but the underlying cause is that no one inside these companies is carrying the deal up to the office that decides. The pattern is not product interest declining. The pattern is executive access never built.

3.
— Pattern weight

Three of the last six closed-lost deals show the same shape.

Castor died at quarter-end last quarter. Ridgeline died Q3. Falconer died Q2. All three followed the same sequence: late-stage operational friction, champion squeezed, EB never threaded, deal slipped at the quarter line. The autopsies are on the record. This is not noise.

⇒
— Recommended move

Run a CFO-facing reframe across all three accounts before the next forecast review.

The reframe is not a sales note. It is a two-paragraph value memo addressed to the economic buyer that names the operational friction and the cost of waiting. Tasha can backchannel on Cordillera; the other two need the direct CFO note. Both can be drafted from the existing template.

The idea

Briefing UI. The surface is an operational briefing, not a dashboard. Distinctiveness comes from rhetorical structure: Situation → Implication → Pattern weight → Recommendation → Evidence. Numbered statements, serif-led claims, sans-led implications, an evidence stack at the foot. The system shows judgment, not just objects.

The native primitives. Briefing block replaces dashboard card. Situation line replaces section header. Implication line replaces subtext. Recommended move replaces CTA area. Evidence stack replaces table. Decision note replaces modal. The discipline: not everything is prose. Operators still need ledgers and workbenches for the operating work; the briefing is for the moment where the system tells the operator what it sees.

Canon preserved. Slightly warmed paper for the reading feel. DM Serif Display dominant — for the headline, the numbered claims, the recommendation. Public Sans for body. JetBrains Mono for the section labels, the timestamps, the deal codes. Orange as the rationed accent — the numbered marks, the recommendation label, the recommendation arrow.
