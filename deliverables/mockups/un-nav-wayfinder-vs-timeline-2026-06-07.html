A. Wayfinder bar

  • Always tells you where you are — trail, here, and the one pull, even mid-task inside a room.
  • Carries across rooms — the bar persists, so context never drops when you move.
  • One persistent strip of chrome — small, but it's there on every surface.
  • Best if the operator wants the system's read visible at all times, not summoned.

B. Timeline is the nav

  • Zero chrome — you land in your work, not in an interface.
  • The page is the pipeline — finding is scrolling, not picking from a list.
  • Quietest possible — nothing competes with the work; Ctrl+K is the only door.
  • Best if the operator wants to feel like they're reading their own desk, not driving an app.
