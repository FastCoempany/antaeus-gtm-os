The composition, in discipline

Four systems, four scales, one workspace. They compose because they never fight over the same magnification — and they hold together only as long as each layer keeps its discipline. Overrun any one of them and the page returns to noise.

PULSE · page

Pulses are authored, not logged. Silences are surfaced deliberately. The past compresses; it never piles up.

RIBBON · section

Threads mark time-zones and groups only. Never a ribbon on a single card. Movement, not decoration.

GROUNDED · card

Anchored edges and spines are reserved for cards whose state warrants weight. Grounding everything erases the meaning.

OFFSET · item

Exactly one offset per zone. The single most-pressured item breaks rank; the rest hold the line.

Canon preserved throughout. Bright field. DM Serif Display for headlines, stats, account names, and the system's read. Public Sans for body and controls. JetBrains Mono for ribbon labels, codes, and timestamps. Orange remains the rationed accent — the one dominant move per surface, the offset emphasis, the active-room spine. Every operator-facing string passes the voice rule. The native primitives — Signal · Reason · Move · State · Evidence — are the object model; cards, ribbons, and pulses are how they render.
