SCHEDULE FLOAT · VARIANT MOCKUPS

Reverse the model: always-on schedule surface

The transient toast — that fires once and vanishes — gets retired. In its place, an always-visible surface in the bottom-left that's the operator's schedule control room: see what's queued, click to adjust, hover for help, settings gear to dial down notifications. A fire becomes a secondary state of that surface, not its purpose. Three variants below exercise different points on the design space — minimal pill, rich card, Birdseye-style float.

Shared behavior across all three variants:
  • Mounted via RoomChrome like Birdseye — present on every room.
  • Hover surfaces a tooltip explaining what the operator can do.
  • Click routes to the schedule-management UI (extends the existing ScheduleModal into a full list view).
  • Settings gear lets the operator turn off notifications, help tips, or the surface itself.
  • Fires happen through the surface — visible as a state change (badge, row, color), not a separate transient toast.

VARIANT A

Persistent Pill

A small navy pill — quietly always there. Shows the count of scheduled skills + an orange dot when a fire is pending. Hover → tooltip card explains what it is. Click → opens the full schedule-management drawer.
