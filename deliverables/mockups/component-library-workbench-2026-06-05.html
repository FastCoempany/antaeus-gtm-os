The idea

Workbench UI. Not a page — a worktop. Three persistent zones: Source on the left for raw inputs (Slack, email, CRM facts, notes), Diagnosis in the middle for what the system thinks is happening (Primary risk · Constraint · Pattern), Move on the right for the recommended action. Beneath: a Proof Strip with evidence, timestamps, and source links. At the foot: a Command Slot. The eye learns the spatial logic once and reads every account the same way.

The native primitives. Source tray replaces the card grid of raw facts. Diagnosis lane replaces "AI insights." Move lane replaces the CTA stack. Proof strip replaces the modal "evidence" popout. Command slot replaces the floating action button. The discipline: keep the zones consistent on every page. Otherwise it becomes cockpit mush.

Canon preserved. Bright field. DM Serif Display reserved for the working object's name and the recommended-move headline. Public Sans for everything else. JetBrains Mono for zone names, timestamps, source codes. Orange tints the Move zone and the recommended action.
