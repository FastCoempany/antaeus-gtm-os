80% Inset Console · 15% Ribbon UI · 5% Lens UI.

The base posture is Inset Console. Content lives in shallow wells, inputs feel pressed into the form, the active nav depresses into the rail. The structural connective tissue is Ribbon UI — thin thread headers tie sections together across the page, trailing off into the field with embedded uppercase labels. And once per surface, Lens UI shows up: the system's most important read, rendered as a soft asymmetric oval with the CTA attached. Used sparingly, the lens reads as this is the thing — and the rest of the workspace stays calm.

The composition

Three layers, working at the 80/15/5 ratio. Inset Console gives the entire workspace its physical posture — content set into the surface, never floating above. Ribbon UI does the connective work between sections so the page reads as one continuous operating surface rather than a pile of unrelated cards. And once per surface, a lens shows up to carry the system's most important read — the one thing the operator should look at first.

80%INSET CONSOLE

Wells for content. Sunken inputs. Depressed nav. Raised buttons because they act. The base posture of every surface.

15%RIBBON UI

Thread headers tie sections together. Each ribbon names a group of work and the count of accounts inside it. Color reflects the urgency of the group.

5%LENS UI

One lens per surface, maximum. The system's most important read of the day. The CTA attaches to the lens, not to a card edge.

Canon preserved. Bright field (slightly cooler tint to support the recessed look). DM Serif Display for headlines, stats, and the lens signal copy. Public Sans for body and controls. JetBrains Mono for thread labels, codes, and timestamps. Orange remains the rationed accent — for the dominant move, the lens aperture ring, the action-ribbon highlight.
