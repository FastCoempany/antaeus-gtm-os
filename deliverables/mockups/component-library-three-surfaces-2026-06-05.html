THREE SURFACES · WORKBENCH + BRIEFING + LEDGER · 2026·06·05

Three surfaces, three cognitive modes.

Briefing for judgment. Workbench for thinking. Ledger for memory.

A GTM operating system should not be a gallery of cards. It should give the operator three distinct surfaces for three distinct kinds of work. The Briefing arrives in the morning to tell the operator what the system has concluded. The Workbench is where the operator drills into one account, sees the source / diagnosis / move spatially, and acts. The Ledger is where everything settles into the running record the inheritor reads. The same content lives across all three, expressed in the form each surface requires.

The composition

Three surfaces, one operating system. Not three modes pasted onto one card-grid. Three distinct rendering forms — each with its own native primitives, each serving a different cognitive mode. The same content (Acme, Cordillera, Stonebridge) lives across all three, expressed in the form each surface requires.

01BRIEFING

System judgment. Rhetorical structure — Situation · Implication · Pattern weight · Recommended move. Numbered claims. Serif-led. Where mornings open.

02WORKBENCH

Active thinking. Spatial zones — Source · Diagnosis · Move. Proof strip at the foot. Command slot. Where the operator drills in and acts.

03LEDGER

Durable memory. Ruled rows — Account · Signal · Move · State — with Evidence indented below. Scanned by the operator; read by the inheritor.

The native primitives across all three. Signal · Constraint · Move · Evidence · Owner · Due · State. These replace the SaaS-default Card · Badge · Table · Modal. Each surface expresses them differently — as numbered situations in the Briefing, as zoned items in the Workbench, as ruled entries with sub-rows in the Ledger.

Canon preserved throughout. Slightly warmed paper for the Briefing and Ledger; cooler workbench surface for the active-thinking mode. DM Serif Display for headlines, claims, and account names. Public Sans for body. JetBrains Mono for section labels, codes, timestamps. Orange as the rationed accent for the dominant move and the recommendation marker.
