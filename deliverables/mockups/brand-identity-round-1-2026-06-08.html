ANTAEUS · BRAND IDENTITY · ROUND 1 — THE MARK · 2026·06·08 · INTERNAL PASS

Five ways to stand on the ground.

Antaeus drew his strength from contact with the earth — and the product's whole claim is groundedness: the system that reads what's actually happening. Every direction below works that idea a different way. This is round 1: pick what pulls, name what's wrong, and the next round narrows. All five use the locked face (DM Serif Display, the navy ink, orange rationed); none introduces a new color or typeface.

AThe Grounded Amythology made literal · ties to the edge-rule

A drafted capital A standing on a ground line that extends past its feet — the giant touching the earth. Flat terminals and mitered joins, same construction as the icon set, so the mark and the glyphs are visibly one hand. The crossbar sits low, like a gauge reading. The strongest mythology tie and the most ownable shape.

CThe Trailwhere you were → here → the move

The Wayfinder's three positions as a mark: a quiet dot (where you were), a square (here), and the orange stroke pulling forward (the move). It's the product's core promise — the system hands you the next move — drawn in three elements. Reads modern and directional; the least tied to the mythology, the most tied to the mechanism.

How to react to round 1pick · kill · steer

Name the one or two that pull, the ones that are dead, and anything that's wrong in the survivors (too heavy, too literal, wrong feeling at small size). Round 2 takes the survivors through refinement — weights, proportions, the favicon at real size, the mark sitting on the landing page and in the Wayfinder corner. My own read, for what it's worth: A is the strongest single direction (mythology + the icon construction + ownable at every size), D is A with more authority and more weight, and E is the honest minimal fallback that can ship tomorrow and never embarrasses. B and C are real but each leans on context the logo won't always have.
