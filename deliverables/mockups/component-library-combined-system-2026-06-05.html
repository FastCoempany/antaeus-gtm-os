The composition

Three layers, working together. The base posture is Signal Calm — most surfaces are plain; cards are unmarked until a risk or action appears; the Signal / Reason / Move grammar repeats. The visual signature is Seamline — fine joins between header, body, and next-move zone; tucked tabs; bottom-rule inputs; buttons with an inner-stroke seam. The earned emphasis is Offset Logic — used once or twice per surface for what actually matters today.

BASE · Signal Calm

The default voice of every surface. Plain until something matters. Signal / Reason / Move as the repeated structural unit. Side-glow alerts. Quiet tables.

SIGNATURE · Seamline

The construction detail. Cards built in seamed sections. Tabs tuck into the surface. Inputs carry only a bottom rule. Buttons have an inner-stroke seam.

EMPHASIS · Offset Logic

Reserved. The one row that breaks rank. The eyebrow tag that sits outside the card. The action button that extends beyond its frame. One per surface at most.

Canon preserved throughout. Bright field. DM Serif Display for headlines and stats. Public Sans for body and controls. JetBrains Mono for codes, IDs, and the Signal / Reason / Move labels. Orange as the rationed accent for the dominant move; all canon color roles intact. The voice rule honored — every surface sounds like a peer operator naming what is real.
