The idea

The Pulse Surface. The workspace is organized by cadence, not category. The fundamental unit is a pulse — a moment when something happened. Pulses appear on a vertical timeline. Today's are full-sized at the top. Yesterday's are smaller. Last week's are compressed to one-line strips. A month back is a thin horizon of names and dates you can scroll past to find any specific event.

State is conveyed by rhythm. A deal that pulses regularly is alive. A deal that hasn't pulsed in 18 days shows up as a visible silence in its own row — a dashed marker that names the deal and the length of its quiet. Importance is size; recency is position; state is color; the active pulse shimmers slightly.

What stays canon. Bright field. DM Serif Display for headlines, Public Sans for body, JetBrains Mono for timestamps and codes. Orange for the dominant move, the rest of the canon color roles intact. The voice rule honored — every pulse description sounds like a peer operator naming what is real.
