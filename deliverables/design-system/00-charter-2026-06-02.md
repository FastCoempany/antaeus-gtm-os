# Antaeus Design System — Charter

**Status:** DRAFT for founder review.
**Date:** 2026-06-02.
**Author:** Claude, working session with the founder (mrcoe7@gmail.com).
**Branch:** `claude/antaeus-gtmos-design-system`.
**Scope:** This charter is the philosophical floor of the Antaeus design system. It does not commit any visual decision. It commits the tests every visual decision must pass.

---

## 0. Why this exists

Today's Antaeus has shipped intelligence the founder can rely on — the heartbeat, the Briefing, Phase F bounded self-modification, the orchestration layer, the Outdoors Events discovery surface, the Founding GTM kit, the Readiness verdict. The plumbing and wiring and inference are real.

What it does not have, yet, is a coherent way to **land for a stranger**. The visual layer is at roughly 1.3 of a true design system. Twenty-two rooms speak slightly different dialects. Cards are the default vocabulary almost everywhere, which reads as inventory rather than direction. The vocabulary assumes commercial fluency the day-1 user does not have. Sarah Chen, on day 90, wants to jump; the new user, on day 1, needs orientation; both deserve a product that knows the difference. Today's app serves the fluent operator and frustrates the curious one.

This charter exists to govern the design system that closes that gap — without rewriting any of the intelligence beneath it. The intelligence is the engine. The design system is the way that engine is *recognized, trusted, and operated* by an unfamiliar hand.

This document does not decide what the icons look like, how motion behaves, what density modes exist, or which component library is canonical. It establishes the floor that every later decision in those domains must stand on. It is meant to be cited in PR descriptions and design reviews. *"Does this answer to the charter?"* should be the first question every later commit can be measured against.

## Part I — What Antaeus is

### 1.1 The lead (two beats)

**Beat 1 — what the operator does.**

> Antaeus exists so the founder can see where the motion really is today, take the next move with confidence, and understand more about the motion than they did yesterday.

This is the daily verb. The user-facing claim. Every operator-facing surface in the product serves it. A surface that does not help the operator see, act, or understand is not earning its place.

**Beat 2 — how that is made possible, and what Antaeus is the opposite of.**

> Antaeus accomplishes this through *selective attention you can defend* — the courage to rank, the courage to discard, and the calibration to back it up. This is what makes Antaeus the opposite of a CRM, and the reason the operator can hand it to a first hire instead of explaining it.

This is the mechanism. The category-defining posture. A CRM's core verb is *store* — you put records in so they're retrievable, and the human does the thinking. Antaeus's verb is *select* — the system pays attention for the operator, defends its picks, and discards what doesn't matter today. A system with a defensible point of view is more inheritable than a system that hands the next operator every record. Selectivity is what makes the daily verb possible, and what makes the eventual handoff valuable.

The two beats land separately for different readers. Sarah Chen reads beat 1 and recognizes her workday. She reads beat 2 and recognizes the mechanism she'd want for her successor. The day-1 user reads beat 1 and feels welcomed. The VC reads beat 2 and recognizes the defensibility story.

The lead is **two beats, not two products.** They nest.

### 1.2 The two tests

Every design decision in the system answers two questions before it answers any visual one. Both must be answered. Neither is optional.

**Test 1 — Selectivity you can defend.**

> Does this surface pay attention for the operator, or make them operate a filing cabinet?

A surface fails this test when it asks the operator to maintain it, browse it, curate it, or sort through it. A surface passes when it presents what matters now, surfaces why, and quietly preserves what's no longer in front of the operator. Storage is comprehensive; the surface is selective. The system always knows about the deals not on screen; the operator does not have to. *Selectivity is for surfacing; storage is for everything.* This is the line that separates Antaeus from every CRM ever shipped.

The second half of the test is *defensible*. A system that selects without showing its reasoning is opinionated, not intelligent. *"Why am I seeing this?"* must always have an answer the operator can read. Confidence is paired with calibration. Ranking is paired with rationale. This is what makes selectivity earn the operator's trust instead of replacing the operator's judgment.

**Test 2 — Loyalty to truth, not to the operator.**

> Does this surface show what's actually happening — including the uncomfortable parts — instead of making the operator feel good about their work?

Most software is sycophantic by default. It rewards activity, hides decay, softens bad news, and surfaces the operator's wins more loudly than the operator's leaks. Antaeus is designed to do the opposite: to be harder to fool than the operator is, to refuse to flatter, to surface the deal that's quietly dying before the operator would have looked, to say *"this is not working"* in plain language. *"This system sees what is actually happening. This system is harder to fool than I am."* (Canon §1.) A surface fails this test when it would let the operator feel better than the underlying state warrants. The discipline is uncomfortable, and the design will constantly tempt itself toward softening. The charter exists in part to immunize against the softening.

The two tests are independent. A surface can pass selectivity and fail truth-loyalty (it ranks well but flatters the operator with its rankings). A surface can pass truth-loyalty and fail selectivity (it shows everything honestly, but the operator has to do the picking). Both required. Top-right quadrant only.

## Part II — How Antaeus behaves

### 2.1 Four behavioral properties (every component implements)

These are not graded per design decision. They are the substrate every component carries by default. A component that does not implement them is incomplete.

**Memory / continuity as substrate.** The system knows the operator. It carries state across sessions. The operator never has to re-explain. Pinned context, return paths, focused objects, recent actions — all of these compound. The relationship has a learning curve instead of resetting every visit. Components inherit this property; they do not redesign it.

**Calibrated uncertainty as first-class output.** Every component that expresses an opinion expresses its confidence honestly. *"I'm not sure"* is a designable element, not an afterthought. Confidence percentages, gate-based verdicts, audit envelopes, *"why this is here"* affordances — all of these are species of the same commitment: when the system is uncertain, the operator can see it.

**Time-horizon awareness.** Components know which horizon they live in — today's pressure, this week's pattern, this quarter's trajectory, the inheritance state — and do not collapse them. Different truths live at different scales. A CRM presents *"open opps"* as if every deal were the same temporal object. Antaeus refuses that flattening. The Dashboard is today; the Briefing is the week; Readiness is the inheritance horizon; Future Autopsy is the deal lifetime. Each component is aware of its scale and labels itself honestly.

**Agency boundary.** The system has agency over itself. The operator has agency over their work. The boundary between the two is explicit and gated. The system may modify its own recommendations, observations, defaults, and proposals — but only with operator consent (Phase F is the doctrinal precedent). The system never modifies operator data unilaterally. Components that act on system behavior are gated behind an accept/dismiss surface. Components that touch operator data require an explicit operator action. The boundary is structural, not enforced by convention.

### 2.2 Two outcomes (state the system accumulates toward)

These are not per-decision tests. They are what the product becomes when the lead is honored, the two tests are passed, and the four properties are implemented. The system steers toward them as a side effect of right operation.

**Handoff readiness.** The first serious GTM hire can walk into the workspace on Monday and run the motion without restating anything. Closed-deal patterns, autopsies, working rails, proof outcomes, segment-jump data — all compound toward this state without the operator needing to *"work on the handoff."* The Founding GTM kit is the rendered surface; the accumulation happens everywhere.

**Operator gets better as a side effect.** Trojan-horse skill transfer. Using the rooms makes the operator more capable of the underlying craft — discovery discipline, deal architecture, proof design, negotiation rehearsal — without the rooms ever announcing themselves as instruction. The operator builds the muscle of recognizing what matters because the system pairs selective judgments with their reasoning, repeatedly, in plain language. Defensibility is what makes this work: a system that selects without showing its reasoning makes the operator dependent. A system that selects defensibly grows the operator.

Both outcomes are real. Both are worth claiming. Neither is the rubric for any individual screen — they are what the system *becomes* when the rubric is honored.

## Part III — Where this comes from

The behavioral canon at `CLAUDE.md` Part III is the underlying ground this charter rests on. The design system inherits it by reference and is graded against it. The charter does not restate the canon — it cites the specific commitments the canon makes and binds the design system to them.

### 3.1 The seven behavioral rules (canon Part III §3)

These are binding. Every component the design system ships must pass all seven. They are tighter than visual rules because they describe *operator outcomes,* not visual properties:

1. **One dominant move per screen.** No surface presents multiple equal-weight primary actions. A design that "looks balanced" but ranks nothing is wrong.
2. **Object before controls.** The operator first sees what is in focus, what condition it's in, what pressure exists. Only then the control set. A surface that opens with controls is structured backwards.
3. **State before explanation.** Current state, confidence, pressure, and next move are visible before any paragraph of copy. Operators scan before they read.
4. **Reward truth, not activity.** Visual reward comes from sharper targeting, stronger signal, stronger qualification, better proof, real handoff readiness. Never from clicks, fields, or content volume. A surface that celebrates activity is sycophantic.
5. **Every save must visibly matter.** A score changed, a suggestion improved, a milestone moved, the handoff got stronger. If nothing visibly changes after an operator action, the app feels fake.
6. **No module feels isolated.** Every surface shows where its input came from and what it affects next. Subtle is fine. Absent is not.
7. **Escalation beats clutter.** Visual intensity is reserved for real risk, real failure, real consequence. Presenting all seriousness at once trains the operator to ignore all of it.

The charter binds: a component that fails any of these seven is not shippable, regardless of how it looks.

### 3.2 The strongest behavioral levers (canon Part III §4)

Seven levers, in effect-size order: prefilled context · ranked next move · visible consequence · explicit progress · save-state trust · contextual handoff · restrained motion.

The charter binds: features should leverage as high on this list as possible. A design that ranks low on this list — say, requires the operator to retype known context — is *intentionally* leaving leverage on the table.

### 3.3 The fourteen principles, ranked by evidence (canon Part III §5)

The design system is grounded in psychological research with effect sizes attached. This is the moat. Most design systems are visually opinionated and behaviorally generic. Antaeus's design system is behaviorally opinionated with the evidence to back it up.

**Tier 1 (high confidence, high applicability):** Implementation Intentions (d = 0.65, 94-study meta-analysis), Goal Gradient, Commitment & Consistency (4.5× compliance lift), Cognitive Load.

→ *Specific contextual next actions in if-then form, never generic "follow up."*
→ *Milestone-based progress reveals the next milestone, not a generic completion bar.*
→ *Onboarding uses escalating micro-commitments; first ask is genuinely low-friction.*
→ *≤5 primary items in any decision zone; reduce extraneous load.*

**Tier 2 (strong evidence, requires careful application):** Endowed Progress (82% completion lift), Loss Aversion + IKEA (63% premium on assembled items), Ovsiankina (not Zeigarnik — the memory component is debunked; the resumption drive remains).

→ *Onboarding converts "not started" into "underway" immediately.*
→ *Cross-object data flow must be visible — the operator must see their work has tentacles.*
→ *Never "all done." Loop transformation, not closure. Resumption is designed for.*

**Tier 3 (powerful, ethical guardrails required):** Variable Reinforcement (only as *insights,* never as *rewards* — no infinite scroll, no notification farming), Self-Determination Theory (no points/badges/leaderboards; competence-affirming feedback only), Peak-End Rule (every session has a designed peak and a designed end; never end on error), Temporal Discounting (visual aging on stale objects; selective alarm to avoid fatigue).

The charter binds: every component the design system ships should map its behavioral mechanism to at least one principle on this list, with the principle's evidence base named. A component that cannot answer *"which lever does this pull?"* is decorative.

### 3.4 The hallway problem (canon Part III §6)

The architectural problem the product is solving is the hallway — too many equal-weight doors, the operator repeatedly deciding *"where do I go now."* The old sequence was `module → page → local controls`. The new sequence is `command → object → lens → downstream loop`.

The design system binds: every surface is graded against whether it strengthens the new sequence or revives the hallway. A palette of rooms presented as equal destinations is hallway behavior. A surface that summons rooms in service of an object the operator is acting on is the new sequence. **Rooms are infrastructure, not navigation.**

### 3.5 The voice rule (canon Part III §11)

> Stop reaching for a single noun to do the work of a sentence. When the urge to write *"the wedge"* or *"the verdict"* or *"the move"* hits, write the sentence out instead.

The design system binds: every operator-facing string in every component passes the voice rule. Voice is not a guideline; it is a structural property of every component. This becomes the *voice-as-component* commitment that the design system will codify in its own spec (downstream of this charter). The Briefing's synthesis-through-the-voice-document is the working precedent — the Briefing speaks Antaeus by structure, not by convention. The design system generalizes that posture across every room.

## Part IV — What this charter does not decide

### 4.1 Design system vs. brand identity (two tracks, sequenced)

This charter governs the **design system** — the reusable primitives and the behavioral contracts they carry. The design system is the floor of consistency: how every component behaves, what every empty state says, how every loading state expresses itself, how every error degrades gracefully.

The **brand identity** is a separate track: the proprietary distinctiveness layered on top — the iconography, the motion language, the wordmark in motion, the surface that survives compression in a tweet, the moment of recognition. Brand identity gets its own charter, authored after the design system's primitives are stable. *Brand without system is decoration; system without brand is generic.* The two tracks are independent but sequenced: system first, identity second.

Both tracks live in `deliverables/design-system/`. The brand-identity charter will become a sibling document when the time comes.

### 4.2 What this charter explicitly does not commit

The following are *downstream* of the charter, not in it:

- **Iconography direction.** A proprietary icon set is a brand-identity commitment with structural implications. The system charter sets the behavioral contract every icon must implement (semantic, never decorative; readable without labels when the operator is fluent). The visual style itself comes later.
- **Motion language.** Canon Part II §6 establishes that motion is "sparse, consequential, and state-based." The charter binds: motion serves state change, not entertainment. Specific motion primitives (timing curves, choreography, system rhythm) belong to a separate motion spec.
- **Density gradient.** The Sarah-vs-new-user tension resolves through density, not through two products. *Density gradient* is named here as a design-system commitment but specified later — as its own primitive with its own rules for which surfaces respond to it and how.
- **Component library.** The reusable component vocabulary — buttons, cards, rails, panels, badges, chips — gets its own spec downstream. The charter binds: every component implements the four behavioral properties (Part II §2.1) and passes the two tests (Part I §1.2).
- **The "today surface."** The orientation-mode surface the new user lands on, sketched in conversation, is downstream of the design system. The design system enables it; the today surface is an instance of it.

These are deliberately deferred. Locking them in this charter would be premature commitment.

### 4.3 What is deferred and named explicitly

**Compounding intelligence.** *Smarter over time* is a doctrinal commitment that has not yet been made. It is deferred to its own session, addressed once this charter's opening is green-lit. It will likely land partly as a behavioral property (some components learn by default) and partly as an outcome (the system as a whole gets sharper over months), but the commitments it implies are doctrinal enough that they should not be pre-sorted. **This deferral is intentional and named so future sessions do not treat it as forgotten.**

## Part V — How we'll know it's working

A design system this opinionated needs its own grading rubric, separate from the per-decision tests. Three signals would tell us the charter is doing its job:

**1. A stranger can walk in.** The product's first impression — the first 90 seconds — orients an operator who has never owned a CRM login. They are not assaulted by twenty unfamiliar destinations. They are not staring at vocabulary they don't speak. They understand what to do next. This is the commercial viability test that canon §1 names: *"the user thinks 'I know what this is. I know why it matters. I know what happens next.'"*

**2. A fluent operator can jump.** Sarah Chen, on day 90, opens the product and moves at her speed — not the new user's speed. She does not have to traverse the orientation thread; she pulls the lens she wants and gets there. The same design system serves her at higher density. This is the density-gradient promise.

**3. The system can defend any visible choice.** Any surface the operator opens, any rank the system shows, any proposal it makes — the operator can ask *"why is this here?"* and get a real answer. Calibration is visible. Confidence is honest. Selectivity earns trust because it shows its reasoning.

The design system is working when those three signals all hold. The day-1 user is oriented. The day-90 operator jumps. The system can be questioned and answers calmly.

## Closing

This is the philosophical floor. The next moves — voice-as-component spec, density-gradient spec, component library, iconography direction, motion language, the today surface, and eventually the brand-identity charter — all stand on this. Each will be drafted as a sibling document under `deliverables/design-system/`. Each will cite this charter and be measured against it.

The intelligence is built. The design system is what makes that intelligence land for a stranger, and earn the operator's trust over time, and be inheritable on Monday. This charter exists so that work happens in service of those commitments, not in service of polish.
