# Antaeus Design System — Lexicon

**Status:** DRAFT for founder review.
**Date:** 2026-06-07.
**Author:** Claude, working session with the founder (mrcoe7@gmail.com).
**Branch:** `claude/antaeus-gtmos-design-system`.
**Scope:** This is the seventh sibling document under the design system charter (`00-charter-2026-06-02.md`). It is the single, complete reference for the words Antaeus uses — the product claim, the sacred nouns, the design-system coinages, the room and family names, the state vocabulary, the words that are banned, and the conventions for capitalization, numbers, dates, money, and time. The voice spec (`01`) is the *enforcement* — the validator that blocks a bad string at the build. This is the *reference* — the human-readable consolidation a writer, a builder, or a future session reads to know what we call things and what we never call them. Where the two overlap, the voice spec is the operational form and this is the prose form; a change to one routes through founder approval into both.

---

## 0. Why this exists

The vocabulary has been accumulating across the charter, the canon, the other six specs, and the commit history — the sacred nouns in canon §2, the coinages in `03`–`06`, the banned list in `01` §2.2, the claim in `01` §2.6, the conventions scattered through `03` §4.10 and the canon timezone decision. A session that needs to know whether we say "gauge" or "spine," whether "pipeline" is allowed here, how to render a dollar figure, or what the locked claim is, currently has to reassemble it from a dozen places. That reassembly is where drift gets in. The session that just spent its length renaming "signal spine" to "the gauge" and scrubbing "earned" from every doc is the proof: a coinage with no single home gets re-derived, re-named, and re-litigated.

This spec gives the vocabulary one home. It does not invent new words; it consolidates the words already decided so they stop scattering. It is deliberately a reference rather than an argument — short entries, organized for lookup, current as of this date and maintained as the canon evolves.

## Part I — The claim and the positioning rules

The one sentence every operator-facing surface is downstream of, locked 2026-06-07 (`01` §2.6):

> **Name the accounts and deals you're working, and the system reads them — it finds what's moving, tells you which are heating up and which are quietly slipping, and hands you the next move. The product is the truth and the decision, not the storage.**

Three rules protect the claim from drifting back toward the CRM category:

- **"Pipeline" never sits in a promising position.** It is foundational vocabulary and is not retired, but it may never be the subject of a sentence claiming what the product does *for* the operator. It stays where it is plainly factual — "pipeline coverage" in the Quota math, a neutral reference to the operator's set of deals. If "pipeline" is the subject of a claim about what we offer, rewrite the sentence around the accounts and deals and what the system reads on them.
- **The operator watches; the system reads.** Watching belongs to the operator — the watchlist is their hypothesis about who matters. The system reads — it pulls signals on the named accounts and surfaces the one worth acting on. Never "the system watches for you."
- **Name the claim, not a metaphor for it.** Every claim resolves to the sacred nouns and the active verb (reads, finds, surfaces, hands you the move), never to a vague abstraction or a borrowed CRM frame.

## Part II — The words we use

### 2.1 The sacred nouns (canon §2)

The objects the system is built around. Protected; a room may enrich one but never redefine it.

| Noun | What it is |
|---|---|
| **ICP** | the one sharp definition of who the motion is for |
| **Focus** | a named group of buyers under a specific pressure (renamed from the legacy "Thesis," canon §11) |
| **Account** | a named target organization with focus, tier, signals, heat |
| **Signal** | a time-limited event implying commercial opportunity |
| **Motion** | a specific outbound move with route and intent |
| **Call** | a planned or live conversation attached to an account or deal |
| **Deal** | an opportunity with stage, value, pressure, and qualification truth |
| **Proof** | the evidence from a pilot a buyer's boss can act on without you in the room |
| **Advisor deployment** | an ask routed through external leverage |
| **Readiness** | whether the motion is hire-ready |
| **Handoff artifact** | the package a first hire would actually inherit |

In prose these need context — "Proof" bare doesn't anchor; "the pilot results" or "what the pilot measured" does (canon §11).

### 2.2 The design-system coinages

The terms the design system invented for its own building blocks. These are distinctive by intent — fresh, concrete, not deck-speak.

**The four composition systems** (`03` Part II): **Pulse** (the page as vertical time), **Ribbon** (the section thread), **Grounded** (the card with weight), **Offset** (the one item that breaks rank per zone).

**The native primitives** (`03` §1.2), the object model every component renders: **Signal · Reason · Move · State · Evidence**.

**Card devices** (`03` §2.3): **the gauge** (the thin vertical rule on a card's left, in the color of its state — replaced the retired "signal spine," 2026-06-07); **the anchored edge** (the bottom rule, in the color of the work); **the stamp** (the mono, letter-spaced judgment line); **the kicker** (the mono uppercase label); **the stat** (serif numeral plus mono label).

**The un-nav** (`03` Part III): **the Wayfinder** (the bar — its three cells are **trail**, **here**, **pulling**); **Ctrl+K** (the verbs-first interrogator); **the HandoffStrip** (the bottom-of-room routes).

**The today surface** (`04`): **the Brief** (the calm narrative landing), **Spotlight** (the one-object read), **Queue** (the full ranked read) — together **the three reads**; the **week's-reads band** and the **proposal slot** (the lower bands); **the read switch** (the segmented control between reads).

**Data** (`03` §4.5): **the Meter** (the single bounded-ratio bar — the only data-viz the language admits).

**Density** (`02`): **Show me how** (the decompressed state), **Step back** (the fluent state).

**Layout** (`05`): the three archetypes — **single column**, **focal + rail**, **object + controls**; **the frame** (the full-bleed Wayfinder bar over the centered 1200px column); **the reading measure** (66ch).

### 2.5 The state vocabulary (canon §10)

The specific words for an object's condition. Use these, not their softer cousins.

`Ready now` · `Workable` · `Thin` · `Operating` · `Needs intervention` · `At risk` · `Handoff-ready` · `Partial` · `Compounding` · `Still weak`.

**Health treatments** (canon §10, frozen 2026-04-03): **Workspace health** carries `Compounding` / `Still weak` (twelve rooms); **System health** carries the score/coverage/fragility variant (Readiness, Quota Workback); six rooms carry neither, deliberately.

## Part III — The words we don't use

Consolidated from canon §11 and `01` §2.2. The validator enforces this list; this is its readable form.

- **Invented single-noun shorthand:** wedge, verdict, the move, decision-grade, operating truth, command intelligence, field read, loom read, ingot read, recovery cue, output ingot, required correction, operator move, do not use, main risk, replacement pressure.
- **Tired business metaphors (banned 2026-06-07):** **spine** (use the description, or "the gauge" for the component); **earned** / earns / earn-its-place (use "genuine," "holds its place," "belongs," "warranted," "the questions that *won* the next meeting").
- **Marketing-deck language:** AI-powered, world-class, supercharge, trusted by, best-in-class, next-generation, revolutionary, game-changing, seamless, powerful, robust.
- **Sycophantic copy:** Great work, You're doing amazing, Way to go, Awesome, Crushing it, On fire — anything that rewards activity rather than truth.
- **Manifesto fragments:** sentences in series with no subject continuity; aphoristic three-fragment paragraphs; decorative `<adjective>-<noun>` compounds trying to feel like industry terms.

The one controlled exception on record: the charter's "the daily verb" shorthand (`00` §0), explicitly justified there for the charter's pace and scoped to that document.

## Part IV — Conventions

- **Time.** The app operates on **Central time (America/Chicago)**, DST-aware (canon, 2026-06-01). Any wall-clock time the operator reads is Central; label it `CT` where the surface needs the zone.
- **Money.** Compact: `$84K`, `$1.2M` — never `$84,000`. The shared formatter owns this (`03` §4.10).
- **Dates and durations.** Relative where it reads naturally (`11 days`, `Thursday`), specific where precision matters (`Nov 3`). Never `11.0d` or machine timestamps in operator copy.
- **Numbers and counts.** Pluralize honestly: `1 signal` / `2 signals`; a zero count is a real count with its own copy (`0 deals` is not the empty state — `no pipeline yet` is not allowed; "nothing here yet" is). `2.4×` for ratios.
- **Capitalization.** Room names and families in Title Case. The coinages take a lowercase article and a capitalized noun in prose — *the* Brief, *the* Wayfinder, *the* gauge — except at the start of a sentence. State-vocabulary words are capitalized as written (`Ready now`, `Still weak`). Sacred nouns are lowercase in running prose (account, deal, signal) and capitalized only when naming the object-model primitive (`03` §1.2).
- **The voice register.** One voice, seven family temperatures (`01` Part III) — the peer operator with a decade of B2B sales scars, in plain English, declarative over hedged, specific over general. Read every operator-facing string aloud; if you would not say it across the table, it is not plain enough yet (canon §11).

## Part V — Maintenance, citations, signals

### 5.1 How the lexicon stays canon

This document is the readable consolidation; the binding enforcement lives in `src/lib/voice/` (the validator, the banned list, the blessed labels) and in the canon (§2 nouns, §10 states, §11 voice). When a word is added, retired, or renamed, the change is made in the enforcement first (a founder-approved pull request, per `01` §2.2) and reflected here. This document never holds a word the enforcement does not; if they disagree, the enforcement wins and this gets corrected. A future session updates this lexicon as part of any vocabulary change, the way the spine→gauge and earned-ban sweep on 2026-06-07 updated the voice spec and should update this.

### 5.2 Citations

Canon §2 (sacred nouns), §4 (rooms), §10 (state vocabulary, health taxonomy), §11 (voice rule, the daily-verb exception). Voice spec `01` §2.2 (banned list), §2.6 (the claim, positioning rules), Part III (family temperatures). Component library `03` §1.2 (primitives), §2.3 (the gauge), §4.5 (the Meter), §4.10 (formatting). Density `02` (Show me how / Step back). Layout `05` §3 (archetypes), §1.3 (reading measure). Surface patterns `06` Part II (per-family). Today surface `04` (the Brief, the reads, the bands).

### 5.3 Signals the lexicon is doing its job

1. **One lookup, not a reassembly.** A writer or session can answer "what do we call this / is this word allowed / how do I format this" from this one document.
2. **No re-coining.** A coined term (the gauge, the Brief) is never re-named or re-derived because its home is unambiguous.
3. **No banned word slips in.** New copy and new specs carry none of Part III's words; the validator and this reference agree.
4. **Conventions hold.** Money, dates, time, and counts render the same way everywhere because the convention has one statement.
5. **It stays current.** Every vocabulary change updates both the enforcement and this reference in the same pull request; the two never drift.

## Closing

A product's vocabulary is part of its foundation. The words are the product's surface to the operator and its shared language to the people who build it, and a vocabulary with no single home gets re-derived, re-named, and re-litigated until the product speaks in several voices at once. This lexicon is the one home: the claim, the nouns we protect, the terms we coined, the words we banned, the conventions we hold. It invents nothing; it consolidates what the charter and its siblings already decided, so that the next time someone reaches for a banned word or lets "pipeline" make a promise, there is one place that already says no.
