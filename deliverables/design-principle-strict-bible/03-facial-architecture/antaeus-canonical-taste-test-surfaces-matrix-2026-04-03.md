# Antaeus Canonical Taste-Test Surfaces Matrix

Date: 2026-04-03

Status: canonical first-set matrix for facial-architecture taste testing

Purpose: choose the first surfaces that will define the visible future of Antaeus before further broad UI rewiring continues.

This matrix is the opening move in the full visible rebuild of the product.

These are not the only important surfaces.

They are the smallest set that can prove whether the visible language is actually converging.

The governing rule still applies:

**rewrite the face, not the mind**

---

## 1. Why These Surfaces

The first set must cover:

- entry
- command
- live execution
- diagnosis
- synthesis

If these do not converge, the rest of the product will keep drifting.

If these do converge, the remaining rooms can inherit the family rules with far less risk.

---

## 3. Required Variant Count

Each canonical surface should get:

- at least 2 variants
- at most 3 variants

The goal is not endless exploration.

The goal is controlled comparison.

---

## 4. What Each Variant Must Hold Constant

Across variants, keep constant:

- the room brain
- the core object/system truth
- the action path
- the underlying logic
- the continuity behavior

Only vary:

- composition
- hierarchy
- plane count
- text burden
- shell/rail presence
- health block posture
- use of type and color emphasis

Do not vary:

- room intelligence
- framework meaning
- strategic logic
- compounding behavior
- room purpose

---

## 5. What Each Variant Must Answer

Every variant must answer:

1. what do I notice first
2. what is the one dominant move
3. what do I understand without reading much
4. how much box structure is actually necessary
5. how much shell is actually necessary
6. what can disappear without harming substance
7. does this feel like a premium operating instrument

---

## 6. Selection Standard

The winning variant is not:

- the prettiest by itself
- the most minimal
- the most original

The winning variant is:

- the one that best satisfies the [facial architecture rubric](/c:/AppDev/v1AntaeusApp/Appv2_290126/deliverables/plans/antaeus-facial-architecture-rubric-2026-04-03.md)
- while preserving the room brain
- and making the product feel more inevitable, desirable, and distinct

---

## 7. Sequence After These Six

Once the six canonical surfaces are taste-tested and winners are chosen:

1. lock the visible rules they establish
2. map the rest of the rooms to the composition-family system
3. rewire production from those winners outward across the visible product

That order matters.

The canonical set is meant to stop further drift, not create more of it.
