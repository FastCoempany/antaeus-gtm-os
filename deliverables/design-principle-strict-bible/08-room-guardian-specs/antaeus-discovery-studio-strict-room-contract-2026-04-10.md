## Room identity

Discovery Studio is a live discovery operating console.
It is not:

- a framework gallery
- a methodology viewer
- a note-taking page
- a poster page
- a static script wall

The room exists to help the rep:

- know where they are in the call
- know what to say now
- know what the buyer might say next
- branch without losing the thread
- recover from interruption, resistance, or time cuts
- capture earned truth
- secure the next move
- hand the truth into the next room

## Primary room law

Discovery Studio is a vertical, collapsible, branch-driven call rail.

The rep lives in one open segment at a time.
Everything else stays compressed but jumpable.

The room must feel like running a conversation worksheet under live pressure.
It must not feel like browsing a page.

## Build size law

Discovery Studio may become the richest runtime in the app.
It may not become the sloppiest file in the app.

The room can be the biggest system.
It cannot be a single-file monster.

## The vertical collapsible segment model

### What a segment is

A segment is one live call state.
It is not a decorative section or a content bucket.

Every segment must answer:

- what is happening here
- what the rep says here
- what the buyer might say back
- what signal to listen for
- what truth gets earned here
- what recovery exists if this breaks
- what move becomes available next

### What every segment must contain

Every segment must contain:

- `segmentId`
- `segmentLabel`
- `timePosition`
- `objective`
- `sayThisNow`
- `buyerMightSay[]`
- `sayNext[]`
- `listenFor[]`
- `earnedTruth`
- `failureRisk`
- `localRecovery[]`
- `skipAheadHandlers[]`
- `markWorkedTarget`
- `markLearnedTarget`
- `nextSegmentRules`

### Segment display states

Every segment must support these visible states:

- `collapsed`
  - shows step name
  - status
  - top missing truth or top earned truth
  - whether it is pending, active, worked, fragile, skipped, or blocked
- `expanded`
  - shows live call language
  - active fork options
  - signal capture points
  - recovery moves
  - next transition rules
- `worked`
  - shows this segment produced useful language or learned truth
- `fragile`
  - shows this segment is not complete enough to trust downstream
- `blocked`
  - shows buyer resistance, missing stakeholder, timing issue, or proof gap

Only one primary segment should be fully expanded by default.
The rep may manually open another, but the room should always maintain a clear active lead segment.

## The fixed segment spine

Every framework runs on the same vertical grammar even when content changes.

The fixed segment spine is:

1. `opening-frame`
2. `current-state-truth`
3. `pain-and-consequence`
4. `trigger-and-urgency`
5. `stakeholder-and-ownership`
6. `proof-threshold`
7. `current-vendor-and-displacement`
8. `decision-architecture`
9. `next-step-lock`
10. `post-call-routing`

### Segment 1: Opening frame

Must include:

- permission bridge
- hypothesis-led opener
- cold, warm, referred, executive, and recovery entry variants
- immediate interruption handler

### Segment 2: Current-state truth

Must include:

- what they use now
- who uses it
- what is manual
- what is systemized
- what still works well enough to defend
- what is brittle but tolerated

### Segment 3: Pain and consequence

Must include:

- what breaks
- how often
- who feels it
- what it costs
- what happens if nothing changes

### Segment 4: Trigger and urgency

Must include:

- why now
- what changed
- whether urgency is real, borrowed, fake, or absent
- whether timing is political, operational, external, or budgetary

### Segment 5: Stakeholder and ownership

Must include:

- who owns the problem
- who feels the pain
- who approves
- who blocks
- who carries post-purchase change burden

### Segment 6: Proof threshold

Must include:

- what proof the buyer needs
- whether they need demo, pilot, benchmark, architecture, reference, security, or operational proof
- whether the proof need is technical, financial, political, or procedural

### Segment 7: Current vendor and displacement

Must include:

- incumbent software, internal build, spreadsheet, or ignore-it motion
- what they like about the current state
- what they dislike
- switching cost
- migration anxiety
- contract timing

### Segment 8: Decision architecture

Must include:

- budget owner
- approval path
- legal/security/procurement gates
- committee structure
- what meeting has to happen after this one

### Segment 9: Next-step lock

Must include:

- exact next action
- date
- owner
- attendees
- reason this meeting should happen
- truth that still must be earned before that next step

### Segment 10: Post-call routing

Must include:

- what goes to Deal Workspace
- what goes to Call Planner
- what goes to Future Autopsy
- what goes into the exportable brief

## Fork model

Discovery Studio must support two fork classes at all times.

### Local forks inside a segment

Every segment must support local branching for at least:

- direct answer
- vague answer
- skeptical answer
- enthusiastic answer
- defensive answer
- misaligned answer
- no-answer / dodge

These forks change the immediate line, capture logic, and next segment rule.

### Track-level forks

These are not cosmetic.
They change the rest of the call burden of proof, tone, and route logic.

The room must support:

- engaged track
- guarded track
- skeptical track
- deflecting track
- compressed-time track
- incumbent-displacement track
- executive track
- wrong-person / redirect track

The rep must always be able to tell:

- which track they are currently in
- why the track changed
- what that changes downstream

## Edge cases and scenario coverage

The room is incomplete unless every active segment can handle these moments without leaving the main conversation engine:

- buyer answers clearly
- buyer answers vaguely
- buyer resists the premise
- buyer challenges credibility
- buyer says they only have two minutes
- buyer asks pricing too early
- buyer asks for proof too early
- buyer asks for a demo too early
- buyer asks how it works
- buyer asks who else uses it
- buyer asks about implementation
- buyer asks about security or legal
- buyer says send info
- buyer says wrong person
- buyer says not a priority
- buyer says they already have a vendor
- buyer says they built it internally
- buyer says they tried this before
- buyer says call back later
- buyer becomes political
- buyer becomes rambling
- buyer becomes silent
- buyer introduces a new stakeholder

## Global persistent rails

The vertical flow is the main canvas.
These rails stay reachable around it:

- `framework rail`
- `segment rail`
- `recover-the-call rail`
- `learned truth ledger`
- `worked memory`
- `next-step docket`
- `support dossier`

These are runtime controls.
They are not decorative sidebars.

## Framework rail contract

The framework rail must:

- expose all 9 product categories
- make the active framework obvious
- switch frameworks fast
- preserve current learned truth when appropriate
- never become a gallery of category cards

## Segment rail contract

The segment rail is the main room spine.

It must:

- run vertically
- keep only one primary segment open by default
- allow direct jumping to any segment
- show compressed status on unopened segments
- preserve resumption state after interruption
- reflect worked, fragile, blocked, and complete states

## Recover-the-call rail contract

Recover-the-call is a sticky global rescue system.
It must slide with the rep as they move down the room.

It must include explicit rescue routes for:

- vague answer
- resistance to the hypothesis
- proof requested too early
- pricing requested too early
- demo requested too early
- send-info brush-off
- bad timing
- wrong person
- incumbent defense
- no budget
- no priority
- credibility challenge
- rep lost the thread

Each rescue must answer:

- what happened
- what it means
- exact line to use
- where that line routes next

## Learned truth ledger contract

The room must preserve earned truth as structured learning, not freeform note clutter.

The ledger must distinguish:

- what is known
- what is assumed
- what remains unearned
- what is important enough to hand off

## Worked-memory contract

`Mark worked` may not be cosmetic.

Clicking it must:

- write a successful move to worked memory
- attach that move to framework, segment, and track
- make it retrievable later in the room
- visually confirm the state change

If a control says `mark worked` and does not change room state, it fails the contract.

## Support dossier contract

Support intelligence must stay reachable but quiet.

It must contain:

- product-category proof anchors
- deployment or implementation anchors
- proof-threshold support
- objection counters
- inbound-question handlers

It must never become the main canvas.

## Inbound-question contract

When the buyer asks an inbound question mid-flow, the room must help the rep do one of four things:

- answer directly
- answer partially and bridge back
- trade the answer for information
- defer with a justified next step

The rep must not have to leave the room or improvise the strategy.

## Next-step docket contract

The room must visibly pull toward a real next step.

A next step is not valid unless it contains:

- date
- owner
- attendees or target stakeholder
- purpose
- reason it follows from the learned truth

## Handoff contract

Discovery Studio must hand structured truth into neighboring rooms without restatement.

### Deal Workspace

Send:

- active framework
- learned facts
- stakeholder map
- current-state method
- trigger event
- proof threshold
- blockers
- next-step lock

### Call Planner

Send:

- active framework
- current segment context
- top hypothesis
- top open questions
- likely objections
- target stakeholder map

### Future Autopsy

Send:

- failure-pattern clues
- unresolved proof gaps
- stalled-next-step evidence
- disqualification or drift indicators

## Visual behavior laws

Discovery Studio must obey these visible laws:

- no giant conceptual headlines above the fold
- no poster typography that harms live use
- no decorative chips
- no fake status accents
- no repeated summary cards
- no layout ornament that slows call-running
- no horizontal sprawl that breaks the call sequence
- no content blocks that do not advance the call

The room must answer live pressure with control, not density.

## What changes by framework

The machine stays constant.
Framework content changes.

The following may vary by framework:

- opener hypothesis
- likely buyer responses
- objection set
- proof threshold
- stakeholder patterns
- disqualifiers
- switching-cost logic
- next-room routes
- support dossier content
- time-pressure shortcuts

The following may not vary by framework:

- the vertical segment grammar
- the need for branchability
- the need for recovery
- the need for visible resumption state
- the need for next-step locking

## Implementation decomposition

The rebuild should be implemented as a system, not as one swollen template.

Minimum build decomposition:

- framework registry/data
- segment engine
- local fork engine
- track engine
- recovery library
- support dossier library
- worked-memory store
- handoff payload builder
- live room renderer

## QA tests

Discovery Studio fails if any of the following are false:

- all 9 product categories are present and reachable
- the framework selector behaves operationally, not decoratively
- the room runs as a vertical collapsible segment rail
- only one primary segment is expanded by default
- the rep can jump segments without losing context
- the rep can recover from interruption in one move
- the rep can enter compression without losing next-step control
- the rep can see what to say without reading a paragraph wall
- the rep can see what the buyer might say and branch from it
- `mark worked` writes real state
- `recover the call` remains reachable while scrolling
- learned truth, worked memory, and next-step state survive segment movement
- support intelligence stays reachable without taking over the page
- the room hands off cleanly into Deal Workspace, Call Planner, and Future Autopsy

## Final lock

Before any live rebuild continues, Discovery Studio must be treated as:

- one vertical call rail
- one segment engine
- one branch machine
- one recovery system
- one learned-truth ledger
- one next-step lock

If a new UI object does not help the rep say, hear, branch, recover, lock, or hand off, it does not belong in the room.
