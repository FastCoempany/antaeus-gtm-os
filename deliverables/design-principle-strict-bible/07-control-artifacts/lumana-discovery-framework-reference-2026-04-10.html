01 Opening & Agenda Set ~2 min
▾
RAPPORT + FRAME

Build Trust & Set a Clear Agenda

Goal: Establish executive presence. Show you value their time. Get permission to run a structured call. Demonstrate you've done your homework.

✓
[Warm open] — "Appreciate you carving out time today. Before we dive in — I know you're running security across a pretty distributed footprint, so I don't want to waste a minute. Would it be helpful if I shared what I had in mind for our conversation and you can adjust?"
Sets the tone that you respect their seniority and time. Gets consent for your agenda.
✓
[Agenda frame] — "I was thinking: spend the first chunk understanding your world — what's working, what's not, what's keeping your team up at night. Then, if it makes sense, I can share where we've helped similar orgs — and we can decide together if there's a reason to keep talking. Fair?"
Assessment criteria hit: Clear agenda → Executive presence & tone. "Decide together" = consultative, not pushy.
✓
[Pre-frame honesty] — "And honestly — if at the end of this it doesn't seem like there's a fit, I'll be the first to tell you. I'd rather save us both time. Sound good?"
Risk-forward signal. Shows you're not desperate. Builds instant credibility.
⚡ Anti-pitch signal: "This is not a pitch call — think like a consultant." You're being graded on NOT defaulting to product dump. The agenda frame proves you can run a call.
MICRO-RAPPORT

Quick Human Connection

✓
[Role-based rapport] — "How long have you been in the security & risk seat at Horizon? I imagine managing 20+ sites keeps things interesting."
Role-specific, not small-talk. Shows you've read the brief. Opens a thread about scope/complexity.
02 Diagnose the "Why Now" ~3 min
▾
TRIGGER EVENT

What Broke / Changed That Brought Them to the Table?

The prompt tells you: after-hours incidents, blind spots, manual investigations. But you need THEM to say it — and go deeper.

✓
[Open-ended trigger] — "What's happening right now that made evaluating a new approach worth your time?"
Open, not leading. Lets them frame it. Whatever they say first is usually what hurts most.
✓
[Push deeper] — "You mentioned [X]. Was there a specific incident that was the tipping point — or is this more of a slow burn that finally hit critical mass?"
Framing skill: "Tipping point vs. slow burn" is a reframe that forces them to self-diagnose urgency.
✓
[CFO pressure probe] — "And I imagine with the CFO pushing cost-efficient modernization across the board — does that create more urgency for you, or more friction?"
Risk-forward. You're naming the tension between "we need to act" and "we need to justify spend." This is a sophisticated question.
✓
[Risk of inaction] — "If nothing changes in the next 6-12 months, what happens? Does the situation stabilize, or does it get worse?"
CRITICAL: This forces them to articulate cost of doing nothing. If they say "it gets worse" — that's your urgency anchor for the rest of the call.
⚡ Lumana tie-back (hold for now): Camera-agnostic = no rip-and-replace = CFO-friendly modernization. You'll use this later, not here. Right now, just diagnose.
03 Current Security Posture & Workflow Diagnosis ~4 min
▾
INFRASTRUCTURE

Camera, VMS & Technology Stack

✓
[Camera landscape] — "Walk me through the camera environment. You've got Axis, Honeywell, some OEMs — roughly how many cameras total across all sites? And are they all IP-based, or do you still have analog in the mix?"
You already know the brands. Showing that = homework done. The analog question matters because Lumana needs IP (encoders for analog).
✓
[VMS deep-dive] — "What VMS are you running today? And when you say it's aging — is that a software version issue, a hardware issue, or both?"
Diagnoses: Are they on Milestone, Genetec, Avigilon, something else? "Aging" can mean different things — slow, unsupported, expensive to maintain.
✓
[VMS pain specifics] — "When your team needs to pull footage for an investigation — what does that process actually look like? How long does it take?"
Extract the workflow. This gets at manual investigation pain. Clock the answer — if it's hours, that's a massive Lumana value play (AI search in seconds).
✓
[Network & remote access] — "Can your team view cameras remotely today, or is it mostly on-site at each facility?"
Lumana's cloud access (any browser/mobile) is a differentiator if they're currently site-locked.
⚡ Value tie-back: "Interesting — one thing I'll share later is that our platform overlays on top of what you already have. Your Axis, Honeywell, OEMs — they all stay. The AI layer just makes them smarter."
OPERATIONS

Daily Workflows, Monitoring & Incident Response

✓
[Monitoring model] — "How does monitoring work day-to-day? Do you have a central SOC watching feeds, or is it site-by-site?"
Distributed vs. centralized monitoring model. This shapes the "single pane of glass" narrative.
✓
[Staffing pressure] — "How many people on your team are currently spending time on monitoring and investigations? Is that sustainable?"
Quantify: Number of FTEs × hours × cost = total manual investigation burden. This becomes a Metrics conversation for MEDDPICC.
✓
[After-hours gap] — "You mentioned after-hours incidents. What's your coverage model outside business hours? Is there any automated alerting, or is it all reactive?"
Probes the automation gap. After-hours = when AI monitoring creates the most value (no human watching).
✓
[Blind spot specifics] — "The blind spots across facilities — is that a camera coverage issue, a visibility issue (you have cameras but can't see them centrally), or something else?"
Reframe skill: You're helping them disaggregate the problem. Blind spots could be physical coverage, data visibility, or both.
✓
[False alarm fatigue] — "How much noise do you deal with? False alerts, motion triggers that aren't real threats — is that a thing?"
If yes, 90% false alert reduction is a knockout punch. Clock their reaction.
⚡ Value tie-back: Self-learning AI agents that monitor 24/7, push real-time alerts (not noise), and let you search millions of hours of footage in seconds. That's the reframe: from reactive to proactive.
04 Quantify Business Impact ~3 min
▾
IMPACT QUANTIFICATION

Turn Pain Into Numbers

The prompt explicitly requires: "Quantify business impact where possible." This is where you earn the highest marks — turning abstract pain into dollars, hours, and risk exposure.

✓
[Investigation cost] — "You mentioned investigations are eating up staff time. If you had to ballpark — how many investigation hours per week across your team? And what does that cost you when you factor in the salary?"
Math hook: If 3 people × 10 hrs/week × $40/hr = $62K/year just on manual investigation labor. You don't say this — but you file it.
✓
[Incident cost] — "When one of those after-hours incidents happens — what's the financial exposure? I'm talking shrinkage, damage, liability, insurance impact — all in."
Gets at direct financial impact per incident. Even a rough number creates urgency.
✓
[Scaling pain] — "As Horizon grows — are these problems getting proportionally worse? Like, every new facility is another set of cameras, another blind spot?"
Future-state framing. If the answer is yes, the problem compounds. Makes the case for a scalable platform.
✓
[Reputation/compliance risk] — "Beyond the operational cost — is there any exposure on the compliance or liability side? I ask because some orgs in distribution have found that aging security infrastructure creates gaps that auditors or insurers flag."
Risk-forward: Opens a thread they may not have considered. Compliance risk can accelerate timelines faster than operational pain.
✓
[VMS maintenance cost] — "What does it cost you to keep the current VMS running? Maintenance contracts, server hardware, IT support time?"
Legacy VMS = hidden cost center. This becomes a line item in the ROI conversation.
⚡ ROI seed: Typical Lumana customers see 100-300% ROI within 2 years. You don't say this yet — but every number they give you is ammunition for the business case later.
05 Cross-Functional Stakeholders & Decision Landscape ~3 min

DECISION MAP

Who's In the Room? Who's Not But Should Be?

Prompt requires: Explore cross-functional stakeholders (IT, Security, Ops, Finance). You need to map the buying committee without sounding like you're running an interrogation.

✓
[Decision landscape — soft] — "If you decided this was worth pursuing — what does that process look like at Horizon? Is this a decision you'd own, or would other groups need to be involved?"
Non-threatening way to map the committee. "Own vs. involved" is cleaner than "who's the decision maker."
✓
[IT relationship] — "Where does IT fit into this? Would they need to sign off on anything related to network, cloud, or data security?"
IT is always a stakeholder in camera/VMS deployments. Cloud architecture = IT cares. Network changes = IT cares.
✓
[Ops involvement] — "Is there an operations or facilities team that would weigh in? Sometimes in distribution, Ops has strong opinions about what happens on-site."
Distribution = warehouse ops is a power center. Their buy-in can make or break deployment.
✓
[CFO / Finance gate] — "You mentioned the CFO is driving cost-efficient modernization. Would Finance be involved in evaluating something like this — or is it more that you'd need to make the business case and bring it to them?"
MEDDPICC — Economic Buyer probe. Are they the champion who needs to sell up, or does CFO need to be in the room?
✓
[Champion test] — "If this did look like a fit — would you be the one championing it internally?"
Direct champion identification. If they say yes, they're your champion. If they hedge, you need another path.
✓
[Prior attempts] — "Has Horizon looked at modernizing the security stack before? I only ask because sometimes there's history that's useful to know about."
Surfaces: Failed projects, political baggage, competing vendors, prior budget rejections. Gold mine intel.
✓
[Competitive landscape] — "Are you looking at other solutions alongside this, or is this more of an early exploration?"
MEDDPICC — Competition. Knowing the competitive field shapes your positioning. Don't be afraid to ask directly.
⚡ Deployment angle for IT: Lumana's Core device is plug-and-play. No port forwarding. Zero-trust architecture. SOC 2. This is how you neutralize IT objections before they form.

CONSULTATIVE BRIDGE

Connect Their Pain → Lumana's Architecture

Assessment criteria: Framing & reframing. You're proving you listened. Recapping in their words shows mastery.

[Architecture bridge] — "Here's why that's interesting to me. The whole reason Lumana exists is for exactly this scenario. Camera-agnostic — so your Axis, Honeywell, OEMs all stay. The AI layer sits on top and turns those cameras into intelligent sensors that monitor 24/7, push real alerts — not noise — and let your team search across all your footage in seconds instead of hours."
60 seconds MAX. Hit: camera-agnostic, AI overlay, 24/7 monitoring, alert quality, investigation speed. That's it.
✓
[CFO-ready framing] — "And the CFO angle — no rip-and-replace. You're not buying new cameras. You're making the ones you already own dramatically smarter. That's a modernization story that Finance tends to love."
Connects insight → value. You heard the CFO pressure in Phase 2. Now you're showing them how Lumana solves that political problem.
✓
[Social proof — light touch] — "We're deployed across 50,000+ cameras now — Meta, McDonald's, NYU — a lot of organizations with distributed footprints like yours. So this isn't theoretical."
Brief. Credibility. Done. Don't over-narrate the logos.
✓
[Gut check] — "Does that resonate with what you're trying to solve? Or am I missing something?"
Critical. Invites correction. Shows intellectual humility. Lets them add context you missed.

07 Close — Next Steps & Mutual Commitment ~2 min
▾
ADVANCE THE DEAL

Establish Alignment & Secure Next Step

Prompt requires: "Establish alignment for formal evaluation" and "Secure next step with clear ownership and timeline." This is where closing strength gets graded. Be specific. Be confident.

✓
[Alignment check] — "Based on everything we've talked about — does this feel like something worth exploring further? I don't want to assume."
Simple. Gets a yes/no. If yes, you earned it. If no, you find out why now vs. later.
✓
[Concrete next step] — "What I'd recommend as a next step: a 30-minute demo where we use YOUR camera feeds — or similar environments — so you can see exactly how the AI handles the scenarios you described. We could bring your IT lead and anyone else who'd want to evaluate the technical architecture. Does that make sense?"
Specific, valuable, multi-threaded. You're proposing something THEY get value from, not a generic 'let me show you slides.'
✓
[Timeline & ownership] — "When would make sense? This week or next? And would you send the invite, or should I coordinate directly?"
Assessment criteria: Closing strength. You're asking for a specific date and who owns the scheduling. This is control, not aggression.
✓
[Mutual action plan] — "I'll send over a quick summary of what we covered today — the key pain points, the areas of fit, and the agenda for the demo. That way you've got something concrete to share with [stakeholders they mentioned]. Fair?"
Professional follow-through signal. Shows you'll do the work. Gives them ammo to sell internally. "Fair?" closes the loop.
✓
[Risk-forward close — if they hesitate] — "I hear some hesitation — and that's totally fine. Can I ask: what would need to be true for this to be worth your team's time for a deeper look?"
Salvage move. If they go soft on next steps, this re-opens the door without being pushy. It puts the criteria in their hands.
✓
[Hard stop respect] — "I know we're bumping up on time. Thank you for being so open — I got a lot of value from this conversation and I hope you did too. Looking forward to the next one."
Tone: Warm. Grateful. Confident. You're not begging. You're a peer who just ran a great meeting.
⚡ Demo framing for later: Lumana offers instant demos and tailored assessments. 3-phase deployment: Assessment → Deployment → Optimization. Deployment in weeks, not months. Zero disruption.
