What this mockup is defending

  • Object-first, not room-first. The strip carries an account, not a room or a score. Canon §3 architectural truth #1 (object-first, not tab-first) made visible.
  • Plain English, no jargon. No "heat", "motion", "readout", "backchannel", "EB", "pre-mortem", "advance ask", "their boss". Every line is something Sarah would say to a peer at another company.
  • The system reads itself back to the operator. Each row is the room writing what's actually happening to this account, in its own voice but plain.
  • Sacred names anchor. Room names (Signal Console, Deal Workspace, etc.) stay — they're the navigation handles. The descriptive line is what's new.
  • Click teleports with focus preserved. Continuity params (canon §6) already exist; the trail just exposes them.
  • The number doesn't headline. Where ranking matters (workspace fallback), it's prose: "Meridian is your most active, Acme is second" — not "Meridian 67 / Acme 54".
  • Ctrl+K, not ⌘K. The hint matches the OS the operator is on (already handled by the palette's `metaKey || ctrlKey` listener; the rendered hint just needs to follow).
