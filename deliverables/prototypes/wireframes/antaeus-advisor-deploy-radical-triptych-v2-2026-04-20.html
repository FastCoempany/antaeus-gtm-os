Variant 01 / Backchannel Desk

The ask is prepared on a private desk.

Why this is different: the user is not browsing a relationship database. They are sitting at an influence desk with one proof blotter, one advisor rolodex, one ask sheet, and three outcome stamps. It should feel tactile, private, and controlled.

Apex needs a warm authority bridge.

Advisor Deploy should make the operator feel the ask being composed, not merely selected from a registry. Every visible object either prepares the ask, chooses the carrier, or records the result.

Variant 03 / Council Floor

Call the right seat into the deal.

Why this is different: this variant makes the advisor universe feel like a council, not a list. The operator sees who can speak with authority, who is too expensive, who is too indirect, and the exact podium ask that should leave the room.

Only one voice should enter this deal.

The council layout is for moments where several advisors could help, but only one should be deployed. It creates a visible cost of choosing the wrong voice.
