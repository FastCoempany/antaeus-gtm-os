How to choose between the three

1 Newspaper

  • Carries institutional gravity. The product reads like a publication of record.
  • Strongest for founders who want the Briefing to feel like an asset they could quote at a board meeting.
  • Dense, ornamental, traditional typography. Most "premium" feel of the three.
  • Risk: ornament can read as performative if synthesis isn't tight. Voice document must do heavy lifting.
The founder who wants their CI to feel like the FT delivered every Monday.

2 Magazine

  • Carries literary depth. The Briefing reads like an essay rather than a dashboard.
  • Generous whitespace makes long-form synthesis breathe; pull quotes earn their punch.
  • Most restrained of the three — lets the writing carry the weight without ornament.
  • Risk: at small Pattern counts (1-2 surfaced), can feel under-filled. Best when Briefings are routinely 2-3 Patterns deep.
The founder who reads Stratechery and wants their own Briefing to live in that register.

3 Memo

  • Carries insider directness. The Briefing reads like an internal document, not a publication.
  • Most action-oriented of the three. Items numbered, moves numbered, signed off at the bottom.
  • Tightest density — most information per square inch. Best for operators who skim before they read.
  • Risk: less differentiated visually. Can read as "another internal tool" if the synthesis isn't sharp.
The founder who runs on action items and wants the Briefing to feel like a directive.
