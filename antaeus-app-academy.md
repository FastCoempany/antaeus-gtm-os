How This Document Works

This document has two parts that serve different purposes.

Part 1 is the journey. It’s organized by progression level, not by
module. You start at Readiness Score 0 and work through 5 levels until
you reach 80–100. Each level tells you exactly what to do, which modules
to touch, and how many points each action earns. This is the self-serve
onboarding path — a founder picks it up and follows it like a recipe.

Part 2 is the reference manual. It’s organized alphabetically by module.
Every tab, every button, every field, every localStorage key, every data
flow. This is the thing you hand to a developer, a support person, or a
power user who wants to understand exactly how the system works under
the hood.

Read Part 1 first. Use Part 2 when you need to go deeper on any specific
module.

Level 1: Set the Foundation (0 → 25)

Goal: Define who you sell to, set the math, and build your first account
list. This takes 30 minutes and earns roughly 25 readiness points. When
you finish this level, the system knows your ACV, your ICP, and your
target accounts.

Lesson 1.1: Complete Onboarding

This is where the system meets you. The onboarding wizard asks 4
questions that seed every module downstream.

Steps

1.  **Step 1:** Open the app. You land on the onboarding screen.

2.  **Step 2:** Enter your company name. This populates the Playbook
    header and the Handoff Kit.

3.  **Step 3:** Select your company stage (Seed, Series A, Series B,
    Growth). This calibrates expectations.

4.  **Step 4:** Enter your annual quota target and average deal size
    (ACV). This is the most important input in the entire system — it
    determines your win rate assumption, cycle length, pipeline coverage
    target, and every benchmark comparison.

5.  **Step 5:** Select your product category (CX AI, Legal AI, DevTools,
    etc.). This loads the correct discovery frameworks in Discovery
    Studio.

6.  **Step 6:** Click Complete. The system writes your configuration and
    redirects you to the Dashboard.

What this unlocks

- Quota Workback: pre-populated with your numbers

- Playbook: company name, ACV, and stage are set (+5 pts)

- Discovery Studio: category-specific frameworks loaded

- Dashboard: ready to generate briefings once deals exist

| Stage set                 | +1         | Playbook      |

Lesson 1.2: Build Your ICP Profiles

ICP Studio is where you define your target buyer with specificity. Not
“mid-market companies” — a rubric with thresholds.

Steps

7.  **Step 1:** Navigate to ICP Studio (Intelligence section in the
    sidebar).

8.  **Step 2:** Option A: Click “Start from template” and select a
    template that matches your market (Mid-Market SaaS, Enterprise
    FinServ, etc.). This pre-fills the builder. Option B: Fill manually.

9.  **Step 3:** Fill in all 7 fields: Industry, Company Size, Geography,
    Buyer Persona, Core Pain, Trigger, and Proof Window. Each field
    should be specific enough that you could build a list of 50 accounts
    matching the criteria.

10. **Step 4:** Review the auto-generated ICP Statement and Buying
    Group. Edit if needed.

11. **Step 5:** Click Save ICP Profile.

12. **Step 6:** Repeat for 2 more profiles (3 total). Create profiles
    for different segments — e.g., one for mid-market CX expansion, one
    for healthcare compliance, one for financial services AI.

13. **Step 7:** For profiles that have led to qualified conversations,
    check the “This ICP definition led to qualified conversations”
    checkbox before saving. This marks the ICP as “worked.”

Lesson 1.3: Quota Workback Review

Your numbers were set in onboarding. This lesson is about reviewing the
benchmarks and understanding the math.

Steps

14. **Step 1:** Navigate to Quota Workback (System section in the
    sidebar).

15. **Step 2:** Confirm your Annual Quota and Average Deal Size are
    correct. If not, edit them.

16. **Step 3:** Review the benchmark cards that appear below the math:
    Win Rate, Cycle Length, Coverage Target. These are ACV-calibrated —
    they change based on your deal size.

17. **Step 4:** Open Advanced Conversion Settings if you want to
    customize Meeting → Opp rate, Touch → Meeting rate, Show Rate,
    Working Days, Touches per Account, or Cycle.

18. **Step 5:** Study the output math: how many deals you need to close,
    how much pipeline to build, how many opportunities, meetings, and
    daily activities are required.

No additional points earned — the quota-set criterion was satisfied in
onboarding. This lesson is about understanding the system’s math so you
can evaluate your progress against real benchmarks.

Lesson 1.4: Enrich Your First Accounts

Signal Console is your intelligence layer. You’ll add accounts, enrich
them with research, and start building signal-driven priorities.

Steps

19. **Step 1:** Navigate to Signal Console (Intelligence section).

20. **Step 2:** Click + Add Account. Enter a company name and domain.

21. **Step 3:** Click 🔍 AI Research to generate AI-inferred signals, OR
    click ⚡ Deep Research to run live web research via Browserbase (if
    the enrichment server is running).

22. **Step 4:** Review the signals that appear: headline, detail, why it
    matters, confidence score, source.

23. **Step 5:** Verify or flag signals using the ✓ and ⛑ buttons on each
    signal card.

24. **Step 6:** Repeat for 4 more accounts (5 total minimum). Ideally
    add 8–10 accounts that match your ICP profiles.

25. **Step 7:** After adding 5+ accounts, go back to ICP Studio and
    click “Score Signal Console Accounts” to see how well your accounts
    match your ICP rubric.

Level 2: Start Selling (25 → 45)

Goal: Convert intelligence into outreach. Build your territory, start
generating touches across email, phone, and LinkedIn. This is where the
system starts capturing real sales activity. Takes about a week of
actual selling.

Lesson 2.1: Set Up Territory Architect

Steps

26. **Step 1:** Navigate to Territory Architect (Territory section).

27. **Step 2:** Start in the Daily Cockpit tab — this becomes your daily
    command center.

28. **Step 3:** Switch to the Territory tab. Add accounts from Signal
    Console using the account selector.

29. **Step 4:** Define Theses (why you’re targeting specific segments)
    and Approaches (how you’ll reach them).

30. **Step 5:** Tier your accounts: Tier 1 (strategic, high-value), Tier
    2 (target), Tier 3 (opportunistic). The system enforces a hard cap —
    use it. Not every account deserves equal effort.

31. **Step 6:** Review the Intelligence tab for signal-driven
    prioritization across your territory.

32. **Step 7:** Use the Manage tab for swap mechanics and re-tiering as
    you learn more.

Lesson 2.2: Sourcing Workbench

Steps

33. **Step 1:** Navigate to Sourcing Workbench (Territory section).

34. **Step 2:** Use the Query Builder tab to define sourcing queries
    based on your ICP criteria.

35. **Step 3:** Build Persona Maps — map the buying group roles for your
    target accounts.

36. **Step 4:** Review the Prospect Pipeline tab as it populates.

37. **Step 5:** Check Source Intelligence for meta-analysis of where
    your best leads come from.

Lesson 2.3: Generate Your First Outbound Touches

Steps

38. **Step 1:** Navigate to Outbound Studio (Outbound section).

39. **Step 2:** Select an account from the dropdown (populated from
    Signal Console).

40. **Step 3:** Set the temperature (Ice Cold, Cool, Warm, Hot). If
    unsure, start with Ice Cold.

41. **Step 4:** Select persona (C-Suite, VP, IC, Procurement) and
    trigger.

42. **Step 5:** Review the channel and CTA recommendation badges — these
    are generated by the temperature × persona matrix.

43. **Step 6:** Toggle No-Ask mode ON for your first 2–3 touches per
    account. Give value before asking for anything.

44. **Step 7:** Generate the touch. Copy it.

45. **Step 8:** Click “Save to Angles” to save the angle AND “Log Touch”
    to log the outbound activity.

46. **Step 9:** Repeat for at least 10 touches across multiple accounts
    and channels.

47. **Step 10:** Use the Methodology tab to study the 5-temperature
    model and the Content Library for template structures.

Lesson 2.4: Make Your First Cold Calls

Steps

48. **Step 1:** Navigate to Cold Call Studio (Outbound section).

49. **Step 2:** Select an account from the dropdown. The pre-call brief
    auto-generates from Signal Console data.

50. **Step 3:** Study the brief: account name, industry, heat score, top
    signal, mental frame.

51. **Step 4:** Click “Dial — Prospect Answers” (or
    Gatekeeper/Voicemail).

52. **Step 5:** Follow the dynamic call flow. At each stage, read the
    script (which references your actual account/signals), review the
    coaching notes, and select the prospect’s response.

53. **Step 6:** If you get objections, use the objection handler chips
    in the sidebar.

54. **Step 7:** After the call, select the outcome (meeting booked,
    callback, referral, rejected, etc.).

55. **Step 8:** If meeting booked, the system prompts you to create a
    deal in Deal Workspace.

56. **Step 9:** Repeat for at least 5 calls to earn readiness points.

Level 3: Run the Process (45 → 65)

Goal: Outreach converts to meetings. Meetings become deals. Deals get
structured with stakeholders, stages, and qualification. This level
takes 2–4 weeks of real selling activity.

Lesson 3.1: Prep and Run Discovery Calls

Steps

62. **Step 1:** Navigate to Call Planner (Calls section). Paste a
    contact name or LinkedIn URL.

63. **Step 2:** Set the company (links to Signal Console for
    auto-populated context).

64. **Step 3:** Review the auto-generated “why now” angle from Signal
    Console signals.

65. **Step 4:** Check qualification gates as you learn them: Pain
    Validated, Budget Confirmed, Decision Process Clear, Timeline
    Established.

66. **Step 5:** After the call, navigate to Discovery Studio (Calls
    section).

67. **Step 6:** Log the call outcome: Advanced, No Advance, or No Show.

68. **Step 7:** Work discovery moves in the territory frameworks. Click
    the “worked” toggle on each move you’ve used in a live call.

69. **Step 8:** Repeat for every discovery call. Target 5+ logged calls
    to earn substantial readiness points.

Lesson 3.2: Create and Manage Deals

Steps

70. **Step 1:** Navigate to Deal Workspace (Pipeline section).

71. **Step 2:** Click + New Deal. Fill in: Account Name, Value (\$),
    Stage.

72. **Step 3:** Add intel fields: Champion, Economic Buyer, Use Case,
    Pain, Competition, Decision Process.

73. **Step 4:** Add stakeholders: click the stakeholder section and add
    people with roles (Champion, EB, Technical, Legal, End User,
    Executive). Mark each as Engaged or Not Engaged.

74. **Step 5:** Set a Next Step with a date. Deals without dated next
    steps trigger the highest-severity risk cause.

75. **Step 6:** Create 3+ active deals to maximize dimension points.

76. **Step 7:** When deals close, select the outcome. For losses: select
    a loss reason and write loss notes. Loss patterns feed the Playbook
    and the Readiness Score.

Lesson 3.3: Run a Structured PoC

Steps

77. **Step 1:** Navigate to PoC Framework (Pipeline section).

78. **Step 2:** Enter your company name and the prospect’s name.

79. **Step 3:** Select duration (7 or 14 days).

80. **Step 4:** Write specific success criteria and scope boundaries.

81. **Step 5:** Review the 4 auto-generated documents: Scope Document,
    Kickoff Agenda, Readout Agenda, Proposal Email.

82. **Step 6:** After the PoC completes, select the Outcome: Converted
    to Deal, Did Not Convert, or In Progress.

83. **Step 7:** Click Save to Deal. PoC conversion data feeds the
    Readiness Score.

Level 4: Stress-Test & Leverage (65 → 80)

Goal: Pressure-test your pipeline, deploy advisory relationships, and
establish the Dashboard as your weekly operating rhythm. This is where
the system’s compound effect becomes visible — every module is now
feeding every other module.

Lesson 4.1: Run Future Autopsies

Steps

84. **Step 1:** Navigate to Future Autopsy (Pipeline section).

85. **Step 2:** Select a deal from the dropdown (reads from Deal
    Workspace).

86. **Step 3:** Read the autopsy narrative. This is the 45-day forward
    simulation of how the deal dies.

87. **Step 4:** Review the top causes of death — ranked by severity.
    Each cause is specific: “no dated next step,” “no executive
    sponsor,” “single-threaded.”

88. **Step 5:** Complete countermeasure tasks. Each task has a script
    you can copy. Click the checkbox to mark it done.

89. **Step 6:** Check the Kill Switch section. If the deal meets all 4
    criteria (low value, stale, weak qualification, high risk), the
    system recommends closing it with a clean-break script.

90. **Step 7:** Run autopsies on at least 3 deals to earn readiness
    points.

Lesson 4.2: Register and Deploy Advisors

Steps

91. **Step 1:** Navigate to Advisor Deploy (Pipeline section).

92. **Step 2:** Go to the Registry tab. Add your advisors: name, title,
    tier (Board/Investor, Strategic Advisor, Angel, Customer Reference),
    expertise, equity position, and connected companies.

93. **Step 3:** Map connected companies to your Signal Console accounts
    and active deals.

94. **Step 4:** Study the Playbook tab — read the 10 Deployment Moments
    and the Psychology of the Ask.

95. **Step 5:** Use the Ask Builder tab: select a deal, select the
    appropriate moment, select an advisor, review the generated ask
    message and advisor script.

96. **Step 6:** Click Log This Deployment to track the deployment.

97. **Step 7:** Update outcomes on the Dashboard tab as advisors
    respond.

Lesson 4.3: Establish the Weekly Ritual

Steps

98. **Step 1:** Open the Dashboard every Monday morning.

99. **Step 2:** Read the briefing: headline, coverage ratio, at-risk
    deals, recommended moves.

100. **Step 3:** Click “Copy Monday Review” — paste it into Slack, email
     to yourself, or share with your board.

101. **Step 4:** Act on the “Your Moves” recommendations: outbound on
     hot unworked accounts, advisor deployments on at-risk deals.

102. **Step 5:** Click “Autopsy Riskiest Deal” to go directly to the
     highest-risk deal’s Future Autopsy.

103. **Step 6:** Repeat weekly. This is the operating rhythm the system
     is designed to create.

Level 5: Prepare the Handoff (80 → 100)

Goal: Close the remaining gaps in your Readiness Score, review the
auto-assembled Playbook, and export the Handoff Kit. This is the
deliverable the new hire reads on day one.

Lesson 5.1: Readiness Score Gap Analysis

Steps

104. **Step 1:** Navigate to Readiness Score (System section).

105. **Step 2:** Read your Module Readiness score (0–100) and Hire
     Readiness score (0–100).

106. **Step 3:** Click each dimension bar to expand the scoring
     breakdown. Every criterion shows: met/unmet status and point value.

107. **Step 4:** Review the “Highest-impact moves” list. These are
     sorted by point value descending — do the top items first.

108. **Step 5:** Go complete the gaps. Common gaps at this stage: 10+
     Playbook fields (+2), Playbook notes substantive (+2), Handoff Kit
     exported (+2), 10+ discovery calls (+2), all 4 qualification gates
     (+2).

109. **Step 6:** Return to Readiness Score after each gap is closed to
     watch the score update live.

Lesson 5.2: Review the Playbook

Steps

110. **Step 1:** Navigate to Playbook (System section).

111. **Step 2:** Review all 6 auto-assembled sections. Each section has
     a status badge: Live (data exists), Partial (some data), or Empty.

112. **Step 3:** Section 1 (Who We Sell To): verify ICP profiles and top
     Signal Console accounts are accurate.

113. **Step 4:** Section 2 (How We Find Them): check outbound touch
     stats, cold call data, LinkedIn activity. This is the full
     multi-channel picture.

114. **Step 5:** Section 3 (How We Run Discovery): review call count,
     advance rate, worked territories.

115. **Step 6:** Section 4 (How We Close): check deal stats, win/loss
     record, advisor deployment data.

116. **Step 7:** Section 5 (What to Avoid): review auto-generated loss
     patterns. Add manual notes for patterns the system can’t see.

117. **Step 8:** Section 6 (Scoreboard): pipeline value, coverage ratio,
     win rate.

118. **Step 9:** Add substantive notes to at least 3 sections (\>100
     characters each). These are the qualitative insights the new hire
     needs.

Lesson 5.3: Export the Handoff Kit

Steps

119. **Step 1:** In Playbook, click “Share Handoff Kit.”

120. **Step 2:** Select which sections to include (default: all 6).

121. **Step 3:** Write a welcome note for the new hire. This is the
     opening paragraph they read. Make it personal.

122. **Step 4:** Select format: Markdown or Plain Text.

123. **Step 5:** Click “Generate & Download.” The file downloads and the
     export is tracked (+2 Playbook pts, +5 Hire Readiness pts).

124. **Step 6:** The exported file includes: all 6 playbook sections,
     your notes, a Readiness Assessment (score + checklist), and a
     Pipeline Summary (active deals, coverage, at-risk deals).
